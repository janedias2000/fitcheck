  7. Key Activities

  Current best answer: Building the feedback loop (photo in → proportions-aware
  assessment out → gets more personal over time), and deliberately keeping
  onboarding friction below the threshold that killed competitors.

  The critical loop:
  1. User takes a photo in a dressing room or at the mirror
  2. AI assesses the outfit against her known proportions, coloring, and style
  profile
  3. Feedback is specific ("the waist hits at the wrong point for your
  proportions") not generic ("this is a good fit for your body type")
  4. Each session adds signal that makes future advice more accurate
  5. By session 8 (hypothesis), the advice feels meaningfully personal → convert
  to paid

  What killed competitors that you must actively avoid:
  - Manual closet uploading (Indyx, Acloset, Whering — all cited as too much
  effort)
  - Long onboarding before first value delivery (Alle App)
  - Advice that feels like it's steering toward more purchases (Stitch Fix,
  retail-affiliate models)

  Honest flag: The session-8 conversion trigger is the most important product
  design question and it's completely unvalidated. This should be your Week 5
