# Interview 5 — Meg

**Date:** June 7, 2026
**Round:** 1 (Problem Discovery)
**Interviewee:** Meg, 30s, two children (ages 4 and 13), worked in fashion
**Length:** 13 minutes | Phone | Recorded via Granola

---

## Key Quotes

> "I very rarely feel confident in what I'm wearing, but it's mostly a body image issue."

> "I feel comfortable and the most confident when my clothing is not too tight."

> "If I didn't end up wearing it, it's because I probably didn't try it on first. And usually it's something that I've purchased online."

> "Until you actually start wearing the item out, you almost don't know if you like it. You think you do, but something about it can bug you when you actually put it on and wear it out."

> "Maybe if somebody who was skilled in knowing what looks good on certain body types... maybe just having some information about my dimensions."

> "Doesn't matter how good a deal it is. If it doesn't look good, you're not gonna buy it."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Shops alone when possible (4-year-old makes in-store shopping difficult)
- Primary factors when buying: appearance first (non-negotiable), then price, versatility, quality, ease of care
- Online purchases more likely to go unworn due to fit issues
- Confused by "curvy" sizing — didn't realize it referred to waist-to-hip ratio, not general size
- Prefers items that last; occasionally buys fast fashion

**Body type challenges:**
- Narrow hips, thicker waist — opposite of "curvy" sizing
- Post-pregnancy and aging created ongoing fit formula disruption
- Florida heat means breathable fabrics are critical to feeling confident (comfort = confidence)
- Shorts that rode up in the thighs led to item being abandoned even after successful try-on

**Confidence:**
- Rarely feels genuinely confident — names this as primarily a body image issue
- Most confident when clothes are not too tight, especially around waist
- Cotton button-up and linen shorts at Disney World = rare confident moment
- Has fashion background but this did not protect her from the confidence problem

**Potential solution needs:**
- Wants guidance from someone skilled in body-type dressing
- Suggested dimensional/proportional information and specific recommendations
- Uncertain what could actually provide this — "I'm not sure who or what could give me that kind of information"

**Relevance to venture:**
- Former fashion insider who still rarely feels confident = confirms confidence is not about knowledge of fashion
- "Body image issue" is the hard limit the product must not promise to solve
- "Until you start wearing it out, you almost don't know if you like it" — sets realistic expectations for what in-the-moment feedback can guarantee
- Not asking for style education — wants proportions-aware feedback

---

## Target Customer Assessment
**Partial target** — life transition (pregnancy, aging) broke her formula, which fits the profile. However, her core problem is body image, which is beyond the product's scope. Useful as a ceiling signal.
