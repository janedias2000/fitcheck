# Interview 9 — Zoe

**Date:** June 2026
**Round:** 2 (Pricing + Concept Validation)
**Interviewee:** Zoe, 31, unique personal style, smaller budget
**Length:** Phone interview

---

## Key Quotes

> "I know what I like but I still question whether it actually looks the way I think it does."

> "I'd want it to understand my style — not tell me to dress more basic."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Has a defined, distinctive personal style — not looking for style education
- Budget-conscious; wasted spend is painful at her price point
- Questions whether her self-perception matches external reality — "does it look the way I think it does?"

**Pain points:**
- Self-assessment gap: knows what she wants but can't verify if it's landing correctly
- Concern about AI defaulting to generic or "safe" style advice that ignores her aesthetic
- Wants personalization to her specific style, not style normalization

**Concept reaction:**
- Conditional positive — interested in the concept but raised an important product concern
- "I'd want it to understand my style" = the product must learn her aesthetic, not override it
- This is a real design consideration: feedback should be within her style frame, not outside it

**Van Westendorp pricing:**
- Too cheap: below ~$4
- Good deal: ~$8–10
- Getting expensive: ~$15
- Too expensive: above $20
- $9/month: Yes, lean (most budget-sensitive in Round 2 alongside Daraja)

**Relevance to venture:**
- Raises the most important product design concern of Round 2: if the AI gives generic feedback, users with strong personal style will churn
- "Understand my style" = the profile-building feature is essential, not just nice to have
- Pricing sensitivity at $9 suggests $12 may be at the limit for budget-conscious users with unique style

---

## Target Customer Assessment
**Target, with caveat** — experiences the problem, willing to pay. Her concern about generic advice is a valid product risk that needs to be designed for.
