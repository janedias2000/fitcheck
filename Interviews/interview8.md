# Interview 8 — Daraja

**Date:** June 2026
**Round:** 2 (Pricing + Concept Validation)
**Interviewee:** Daraja, 31, new mom
**Length:** Phone interview

---

## Key Quotes

> "Getting dressed for something that matters — I'll go through four or five outfits before I find one that feels right."

> "I send pictures to my sister but she always says it looks good. I can't tell if she means it."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- New mom — body has changed post-pregnancy, old formula disrupted
- High-stakes getting dressed moments (events, going out) trigger multi-outfit cycling
- Relies on sister for photo feedback; aware the feedback may not be honest

**Pain points:**
- Post-pregnancy body shift means she's back in formula-building mode
- The getting-dressed ritual before important events takes significant time and emotional energy
- Knows her feedback source may be polite rather than honest — exactly the problem Fit Check solves

**Concept reaction:**
- Strong positive — the multi-outfit cycling moment resonated immediately
- Excited by idea of feedback that doesn't come with social obligation to be nice

**Van Westendorp pricing:**
- Too cheap: below ~$5
- Good deal: ~$8–12
- Getting expensive: ~$18
- Too expensive: above $25 (strongest ceiling among Round 2 interviewees — most budget-aware)
- $9/month: Yes, but noted it's a real consideration given budget as a new mom

**Relevance to venture:**
- New mom = prime life-transition use case (pregnancy broke the formula, back to square one)
- Pre-event getting dressed moment is a specific high-pain use case; informs "this is for you if" list
- Budget sensitivity is a reminder that even willing buyers have ceiling; $12 may be at upper edge for this segment

**Strong target** — life transition broke her formula, experiences the problem acutely, strong concept resonance. Budget ceiling noted.
