# Interview 3 — Danai

**Date:** June 7, 2026
**Round:** 1 (Problem Discovery)
**Interviewee:** Danai, preschool teacher and business owner, frugal with clothing spend
**Length:** 9 minutes | Phone | Recorded via Granola

---

## Key Quotes

> "I felt very frustrated because my body type is a little bit different — smaller waist, but I have bigger hips and thighs. And so a lot of pants and shorts fit me kind of weird."

> "I felt a little sad at the end of our day because I really didn't find anything that really worked for me."

> "There are times that you're confident at the store, and you get home and then you go to put it on again, and you're like, no, never mind."

> "The store mirror and the lights just made it look like it looked amazing. And then when I got home and actually saw it in real light in my bathroom, I was like, what the hell was I thinking?"

> "I try to find an influencer that has my body type, and then I follow them — she's figured out what looks good on our body type. And then I kind of just go from there."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Primarily shops online — shops alone by default
- Returns often due to fit issues, not wrong size (items fit but don't look right)
- Prefers online for easier UPS returns — has optimized her entire system around returns
- When she finds something that fits well, buys it in every color
- Photo-sends to husband, sisters, or kids when unsure — "if they are hesitant in any way, I just won't get it"

**Body type challenges:**
- Smaller waist, bigger hips and thighs
- Pants and shorts fit "weird" due to proportions
- Recent in-store experience with daughter-in-law left her feeling sad — nothing worked
- Store mirrors and lighting mislead — items look different under home lighting

**Competing workarounds:**
- Follows influencers who share her body type (free, effective, already in-market)
- Optimized return system via online shopping — not in active pain, has adapted

**Confidence:**
- Generally confident getting dressed for work; has a separate "preschool formula"
- Changes after work if going elsewhere — context-specific confidence
- Considers age-appropriateness: "Am I trying to dress too young? Too old?"

**Relevance to venture:**
- The influencer workaround and optimized return system are key competitive threats
- Confirms: in-store mirrors lie, home lighting reveals the truth — this is the gap Fit Check can fill at home
- Wasted spend (kept item she didn't love rather than return) still happens even for an optimizer

---

## Target Customer Assessment
**Moderate target** — has largely adapted through returns and influencers. Useful for competitive insight. Her "confident in store, different at home" experience is a strong product signal.
