# Interview 4 — Mya

**Date:** June 7, 2026
**Round:** 1 (Problem Discovery)
**Interviewee:** Mya, 20s, limited budget
**Length:** 8 minutes | Phone | Recorded via Granola

---

## Key Quotes

> "I can't get a 360 of myself sometimes."

> "I kind of do need people to tell me what it looks like. If my friends tell me it looks good, it hypes me up."

> "I don't want to be so seen in public" — when not confident in outfit.

> "Sometimes when you do ask a friend, they're just trying to be nice, and they won't give you, like, real input on the outfit."

> "My mom is kind of brutal, and she'll be like, that doesn't look good. But then sometimes when you do ask a friend, they're just trying to be nice."

> "Taking pictures and seeing if I like how I look in the pictures. Or FaceTime my mom... does this look good? So that type of thing definitely helps."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Shopping is social — brings friends/family, asks for opinions as part of the experience
- Key factors when unsure: fit quality, occasion planning, likelihood of actually wearing it
- Takes spin videos in dressing room to see herself from multiple angles when alone
- Impulse purchases without trying on lead to regret (Target tube top — hated it, never wore it)

**Confidence:**
- ~40% genuinely confident in regular clothes, ~50% in going-out outfits
- Gym clothes worn frequently for utility, not confidence
- Confidence boosters: positive feedback from others, good photos of outfit
- "I don't want to be so seen in public" when not confident

**Information sources:**
- Mom = "brutal" honest feedback (trusts this)
- Friends = often "just trying to be nice" (knows this is unreliable)
- Recognizes the honesty gap but can't always access the honest person
- FaceTimes mom and twin sister for pre-going-out approval

**Relevance to venture:**
- Highest-pain interviewee in Round 1 alongside Aly
- "Can't get a 360 of myself" is the clearest articulation of the core physical problem
- "Trying to be nice" quote directly validates the product's honesty angle
- Limited budget + 40–50% confidence = wasted spend hurts most
