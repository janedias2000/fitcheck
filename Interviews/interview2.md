# Interview 2 — Aly S.

**Date:** June 7, 2026
**Round:** 1 (Problem Discovery)
**Interviewee:** Aly S., 20s, limited budget, wants to expand wardrobe
**Length:** 8 minutes | Phone | Recorded via Granola

---

## Key Quotes

> "I feel like knowing what kind of styles and silhouettes look good on your body is important, and sometimes it's hard to figure that out in the store."

> "I'm kind of pale, a lot of colors wash me out, and sometimes I'll still pick up a color that doesn't look good on me. And if I knew, like, okay, stay away from this color, I would stop doing that."

> "It just makes you feel good about yourself and you feel confident... like, I would feel fine if I would take a picture in this outfit. I want to go out and be seen."

> "If I'm with somebody, I'd love to ask their opinion. Especially if they're a close friend, they can really give a helping hand."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Key decision factors: does it fit, do I have something similar, can I afford it, is it special enough?
- Online purchases more problematic — sizing inconsistencies even within same brand
- Swimsuits never tried on in store due to discomfort
- Asks close friends for opinions when shopping together

**Confidence:**
- ~50% of mornings feels genuinely confident in outfit choice
- When not confident: compensates with enhanced hair/makeup
- A confident outfit = willing to take photos, go out to dinner, be seen publicly

**Style knowledge gaps:**
- Struggles to identify which silhouettes work best for her body type
- Knows certain colors wash her out but continues picking them anyway
- Wants clearer rules: "stay away from this color"
- Trusts opinions from others alongside her own judgment

**Relevance to venture:**
- Strong target customer — in active trial-and-error phase, limited budget, 50% daily confidence
- The color pattern is important: she *knows* what doesn't work and keeps doing it — she needs a rule enforcer at the moment of decision
- Confirms the universal photo-sending behavior

---

## Target Customer Assessment
**Strong target** — in formula-building phase, low daily confidence, limited budget means wasted spend hurts.
