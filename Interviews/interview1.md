# Interview 1 — Allison G.

**Date:** June 7, 2026
**Round:** 1 (Problem Discovery)
**Interviewee:** Allison G., 40s, larger budget (mindful with spending), tall
**Length:** 12 minutes | Phone | Recorded via Granola

---

## Key Quotes

> "It really took years for me to develop my ability to pick out clothes and know if I was going to wear them... it was really a lot of trial and error, and I've made a lot of mistakes."

> "I understand the sales associate is just trying to make a sale... it's more reassuring when you ask a friend or family member."

> "I have gotten to the point where I definitely like to try on clothes before I buy them. I don't enjoy going back to stores to return."

> "90% of the time I feel confident. I have a formula of different outfits that I feel confident in and comfortable in."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Always tries on in-store before buying; avoids returns by being certain before leaving
- Evaluates: quality, fit, price, and whether it fits into existing wardrobe
- Uses three-sided mirrors to see herself from multiple angles when uncertain
- Sends photos to friends/family when unsure — trusts them over sales associates

**Confidence:**
- 90% confident getting dressed daily — has developed a "formula" over decades
- Will not leave the house without feeling confident; changes if needed
- Formula took 20+ years of trial and error to develop

**Unworn items:**
- Multiple items with tags sitting unworn from online purchases
- Missed return windows on items that fit but weren't loved
- In-store try-on rarely results in unworn purchases

**Relevance to venture:**
- Not primary target customer (already self-solved, 40s) — but represents who the 22–35 target becomes after decades of mistakes
- Confirms: feedback from trusted people > sales associate feedback
- Confirms: the problem is proportions and how something looks, not size
