# Interview 10 — Haley

**Date:** June 2026
**Round:** 2 (Pricing + Concept Validation)
**Interviewee:** Haley, 36, larger budget
**Length:** Phone interview

---

## Key Quotes

> "I'll try something on and think I love it, and then I get dressed to actually go out and I'm not sure anymore."

> "I would pay more if it actually worked — I spend so much more than that on things I don't end up wearing."

---

## Key Takeaways

**Shopping behavior:**
- Higher disposable income — spends more on clothing but still experiences the same uncertainty
- Willing to pay meaningfully if the product delivers value
- Experience the fitting-room-to-real-life confidence gap: confident in store, uncertain when it counts

**Pain points:**
- Gets ready to go out, puts on something she thought she loved, feels uncertain at the last moment
- Has wasted more money on unused clothing (higher budget = higher wasted spend in dollar terms)
- Frames value in terms of what she already wastes: "$12/month is nothing compared to what I've spent on things I never wore"

**Concept reaction:**
- Most enthusiastic positive reaction of Round 2
- Immediately connected the value to her own spending behavior
- High willingness to pay — framed $9 as obviously reasonable given context

**Van Westendorp pricing:**
- Too cheap: below ~$8
- Good deal: ~$15–20
- Getting expensive: ~$25
- Too expensive: above $40
- $9/month: Yes, enthusiastically
- $12/month: Also yes — "that's still nothing"

**Relevance to venture:**
- Confirms the product works across budget tiers — this is not only a budget-constraint problem
- Her "too cheap" threshold ($8) is the highest in Round 2 — low price can signal low quality to higher spenders
- Provides pricing ceiling data: good deal threshold up to $20 gives room above $12
- "I spend so much more than that on things I don't end up wearing" is a potential testimonial/marketing frame

---

## Target Customer Assessment
**Primary target** — high pain, high willingness to pay, immediate concept resonance. Suggests the customer who is easiest to convert on price.
